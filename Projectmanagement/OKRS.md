## Objective

Develop a machine learning model to predict tourist expenditure to identify high-value segments and optimize marketing spend.

### Key Result 1: Technical Success Threshold

#### Goal: Achieve a Mean Absolute Error (MAE) at least 15% lower than the "Simple Mean Baseline."

Actual Result: ~40% Improvement.

Evidence: The baseline error was ~8.1M TZS, which we slashed to ~4.75M TZS using a hyperparameter-tuned XGBoost.

Status: EXCEEDED ✅

### Key Result 2: Identifying High-Value Segments

#### Goal: Pinpoint which factors drive the highest economic impact.

Actual Result: Identified that "Tour Arrangement" (Package vs. Independent) is the #1 predictor, followed by "Purpose" (Wildlife/Leisure).

Evidence: High-value segments are specifically tourists from the USA, UK, Denmark, and Poland engaging in Wildlife Tourism.

Status: ACHIEVED ✅

### Key Result 3: Actionable Marketing Insights

#### Goal: Provide recommendations to optimize marketing resources.

Actual Result: Formulated the "High-Value Activity Conversion" strategy.

Evidence: Data proves that increasing stay length and independent/package have the most influence and not the tourists origin

Status: ACHIEVED ✅
